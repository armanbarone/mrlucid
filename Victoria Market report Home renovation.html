Victoria, BC Market Intelligence

Why Victoria Is One of Canada's
Best Renovation Markets Right Now

$1.1M+Avg Home Value
Victoria homeowners are sitting on some of the highest property values in Canada. When your home is worth over a million dollars, a $30,000 kitchen renovation or a $18,000 bathroom remodel is a logical investment — not a luxury. High home equity means homeowners are willing and able to spend on quality renovations. This is a premium-intent market. The buyer is not price-shopping on Kijiji — they're Googling "best kitchen renovation company Victoria BC" and calling the top result.
57%Homeownership Rate
Greater Victoria's homeownership rate sits at 57% across the CMA, with ownership rates significantly higher in Saanich, Oak Bay, Langford, and Colwood — the primary renovation catchment zones. The median age of Victoria's housing stock is over 40 years, meaning a massive share of the city's homes are due for kitchen, bathroom, and basement upgrades. Older homes = higher renovation urgency. CMHC's 2025 data confirms renovation spending in BC is growing faster than national average.
$24.3BCanada Reno Market
The Canadian home renovation industry hit $24.3B in 2025, growing at a 4.2% CAGR toward $29.8B by 2030. BC accounts for approximately 16–18% of national renovation spend — disproportionately high due to housing values, weather (exterior projects year-round), and an affluent aging population investing in livability. Victoria specifically sees strong demand driven by retirees upgrading older homes, young families buying dated properties and renovating immediately, and income-property investors improving rental stock.
40+ yrsAvg Victoria Housing Age
The majority of Victoria's residential housing stock was built between 1950 and 1990. These homes need kitchens modernized, bathrooms waterproofed and retiled, basements converted to livable space, and outdated layouts opened up. This isn't discretionary spending for these homeowners — it's maintenance, resale value protection, and quality-of-life investment. A contractor who shows up at the top of Google Maps for "bathroom renovation Victoria" is talking to a buyer who is already decided and just choosing who to trust with their home.
